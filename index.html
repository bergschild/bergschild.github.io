My work

autonomous planning and resource allocation under uncertainty

My passion to help businesses solve their issues using AI. My toolkit includes machine learning, optimization and econometrics.

Most recently I have been working on an autonomous customer relationship management system. As described in my recently published paper, the system can learn an optimal direct marketing strategy, using deep reinforcement learning to esimate customer lifetime value of a given customer, conditional on possible marketing actions. The paper won the the best paper award through Advanced Topics in Sequential Decision Making (AA229) class, taught by Prof. Mykel Kochenderfer, Winter 2015.

Preceding Stanford, I spent a year working for Global Client Insights team at BlackRock. There I developed an aproach to optimal allocation of digital marketing budget that was adopted by the marketing department, did work on marketing effectiveness evaluation for iShares ETFs, used Bayesian nets to explore the factors affecting iShares ETF flows and trained Buy-Till-You-Die models to evaluate customer lifetime value of BlackRock investors.

Even earlier, while at Columbia Business School, I wrote a thesis on prediction of the box office results of movies, based on preferences of taste-based viewer communities, supervised by Prof. Kamel Jedidi.

In my free time, I am a fan of film production, law, origami, foreign languages, and philosophy.
